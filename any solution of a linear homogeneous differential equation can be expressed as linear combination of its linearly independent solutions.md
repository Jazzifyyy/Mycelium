# Statement
Suppose $f_{1},\dots,f_{n}$ are $n$ linearly independent solutions of 
$$a_{0}(x)y^{(n)} + a_{1}(x)y^{(n-1)} + \dots + a_{n-1}(x)y' + a_{n}(x)y = 0,$$
(which exist because of [[existence of linearly independent solutions of a linear homogeneous differential equation]]), then *every* solution of the equation can be expressed as a linear combination
$$c_{1}f_{1}+\dots + c_{n}f_{n}$$
of those solutions by proper choice of the constants $c_{1},\dots,c_{n}$.
