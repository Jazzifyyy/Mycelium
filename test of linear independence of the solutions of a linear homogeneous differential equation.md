# Statement
The $n$ solutions $f_{1},\dots,f_{n}$ of
$$a_{0}(x)y^{(n)} + a_{1}(x)y^{(n-1)} + \dots + a_{n-1}(x)y' + a_{n}(x)y = 0$$
are *linearly independent* on $[a,b]$ iff the [[wronskian]] of $f_{1},\dots,f_{n}$ is different from zero for some $x \in [a,b]$.

Further, the wronksian of $f_{1},\dots,f_{n}$ of the above equation is either identically zero on $[a,b]$ or else is never zero on $[a,b]$.
