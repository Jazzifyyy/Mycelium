# Notation
The first-order differential equations may be expressed in either the *derivative form*:
$$\frac{dy}{dx} = f(x,y)$$
or the differential form
$$M(x,y)dx + N(x,y)dy = 0.$$
An equation in one of these forms may readily be written in the other form.

# Example
The equation
$$\frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{x^2 + y^2}{x-y}$$
may be expressed as 
$$(x^2 + y^2 )dx + (y-x)dy=0.$$

**Note:** In the first form, it is clear from the notation itself that $y$ is regarded as the dependent variable and $x$ as the indendent one; but in the latter form, there is know way to know this. 

For the remainder of the notes, for all differential equations of the above form in $x,y$, we shall regard $y$ as dependent and $x$ as independent.
