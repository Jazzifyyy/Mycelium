# Definition
A **linear ordinary differential equation of order** $n$ in the dependent variable $y$ and the independent variable $x$ is an equation that is of the form
$$a_{0}(x)y^{(n)} + a_{1}(x)y^{(n-1)} + \dots + a_{n-1}(x)y' + a_{n}(x)y = F(x)$$
where $a_{0}$ is not identically zero. 

We also assume that $a_{0}, a_{1}, \dots, a_{n}$ and $F$ are continuous real function on an interval $[a,b]$ and $a_{0}$ is not zero on this interval. 

The rhs term $F(x)$ is called the *non-homogeneous term*. If $F$ is identically zero, then the equation is called *homoegeneous*.
