# Statement
The $n$th order homogeneous linear differential equation
$$a_{0}(x)y^{(n)} + a_{1}(x)y^{(n-1)} + \dots + a_{n-1}(x)y' + a_{n}(x)y = 0$$
always has $n$ solutions that are *linearly independent* (on what interval tho?), i.e., if 
$$c_{1}f_{1}(x)+\dots+c_{n}f_{n}(x)=0$$
for all $x \in [a,b]$, then $c_{1}=c_{2}=\dots=c_{n}=0$.
