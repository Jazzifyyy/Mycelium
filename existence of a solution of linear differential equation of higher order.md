# Statement
Consider the $n$th order linear differential equaton
$$a_{0}(x)y^{(n)} + a_{1}(x)y^{(n-1)} + \dots + a_{n-1}(x)y' + a_{n}(x)y = F(x)$$
where $a_{0}, a_{1}, \dots, a_{n}$ and $F$ are continuous real function on an interval $[a,b]$ and $a_{0}$ is not zero on this interval. 

Suppose $x_{0}$ is any point in the interval $[a,b]$ and suppose $c_{0},c_{1},\dots,c_{n-1}$ be $n$ constants. 

Then there exists a *unique* solution $f$ of the differential equation such that 
$$f(x_{0})=c_{0}, f'(x_{0})=c_{1},\dots,f^{(n-1)}(x_{0})=c_{n-1}$$
and this solution is defined over the entire $[a,b]$.
